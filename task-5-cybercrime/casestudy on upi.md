**Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India. It happens when people are tricked into allowing payments or sharing their bank details. This kind of fraud has increased a lot because more people are using payment platforms.

Here's how a typical UPI fraud happens.

**\*** First the scammer contacts the person through a phone call, message or social media. They pretend to be a bank official, customer support agent or even a buyer/seller.

**\*** Next the person is convinced to click on a link scan a QR code or share details like OTP or UPI PIN.

**\*** Sometimes the scammer sends a "payment request" of sending money. The person then unknowingly approves it.

**\*** Once the person allows the transaction the money is instantly transferred to the scammers account.

The main targets of frauds are students, elderly people and those who do not fully understand digital payment systems. The consequences can be very severe. They include loss, emotional stress and loss of trust in digital platforms.

To prevent incidents users should never share their UPI PIN or OTP. They should verify contacts. They should also be cautious while approving payment requests. Awareness and careful digital behavior are key, to avoiding UPI fraud. UPI safety is very important
